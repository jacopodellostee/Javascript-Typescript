Who is Tyler, The Creator?

Tyler, The Creator (real name: Tyler Gregory Okonma) is a multifaceted artist: rapper, producer, designer, and creative visionary. Founder of the Odd Future collective, he has revolutionized the hip-hop scene with a unique aesthetic and iconic albums like IGOR and Call Me If You Get Lost. Known for his provocative and theatrical style, Tyler is not just a musician but a cultural force, capable of uniting music, fashion, design, and humor into an unmistakable identity.

Bastard

2009

Bastard is Tyler, The Creator's debut mixtape, released on December 25, 2009. Completely self-produced, Bastard introduced the world to Tyler's dark, chaotic, and disturbing style, dealing with themes like abandonment, self-hatred, and the desire for redemption. Despite its dark tone, the album became a manifesto for a new generation of nonconformist rappers.

Goblin

2011

Goblin is Tyler, The Creator's official debut album, released on May 10, 2011, under XL Recordings. An ideal sequel to the Bastard mixtape, it continues the narrative with the character Dr. TC, an imaginary figure who serves as Tyler's therapist. The album is known for its disturbed tone, frequent use of alter egos, and lo-fi, dark, minimal soundscapes. The track Yonkers achieved massive success, going viral and definitively launching Tyler's mainstream career.

Wolf

2013

Wolf is Tyler, The Creator's second studio album, released on April 2, 2013. The album represents an artistic evolution from previous works, with more refined productions, jazz and soul influences, and more intimate lyrics. In Wolf, Tyler continues his conceptual narrative, exploring themes like love, abandonment, and identity, while maintaining his humor and irreverence.

Cherry Bomb

2015

Cherry Bomb is Tyler, The Creator's third studio album, released on April 13, 2015. Characterized by aggressive, distorted, and experimental sound, the album divides critics and fans but confirms Tyler as an artist ready to challenge traditional hip-hop boundaries. Between chaotic tracks and melodic moments, Cherry Bomb is an irreverent and bold sonic journey.

Flower Boy

2017

Flower Boy, released on July 21, 2017, marks a turning point in Tyler, The Creator's career. The album is characterized by richer and more curated production, with themes exploring identity, loneliness, and personal growth. It was praised for its emotional maturity and skillful use of soul and jazz sonorities.

IGOR

2019

IGOR is Tyler, The Creator's fifth studio album, released on May 17, 2019. The album was critically acclaimed for its experimental production, distinctive aesthetics, and intense emotional expressiveness. With IGOR, Tyler partially abandons traditional rap to explore synth-funk, R&B, and neo-soul sonorities, telling a troubled and transformative love story. The project was awarded the Grammy Award for Best Rap Album in 2020.

Call Me If You Get Lost

2021

Call Me If You Get Lost is Tyler, The Creator's sixth studio album, released on June 25, 2021. The project marks a return to more direct rap, combining mixtape energy with Tyler's typical curated aesthetic. Featuring legendary DJ Drama, Call Me If You Get Lost is inspired by the Gangsta Grillz tradition, exploring themes like identity, success, and unrequited love, all seasoned with luxurious and versatile productions.

Chromakopia

2024

Chromakopia is Tyler, The Creator's eighth studio album, released on October 28, 2024. This project follows the success of Call Me If You Get Lost and represents a further evolution in the versatile artist's creative journey. The album, entirely written, produced, and arranged by Tyler himself, explores themes related to his childhood and growth, offering a rich sound that blends hip-hop, R&B, and jazz elements, with standout collaborations including Daniel Caesar, Lil Wayne, ScHoolboy Q, and Santigold.
